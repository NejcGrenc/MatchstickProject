The following text supplements the information provided in the interview. Please read the text carefully.

At the outset, we want to emphasize that your participation is voluntary. You can opt out and stop participation at any time without giving a reason. There is no penalty by refusal of participation in this study. There is also no associated health risk with participation in the study. As this is not a sponsored study, there will also be no compensation for participation, however, we are really grateful for it.

By inputting your name and clicking on the green Continue button on the login page, you agree to do participate in the experiments and try your best to complete both studies in a quick and calm fashion.

Goal of the study

Research investigations are a source of new scientific knowledge. In the scope of this study we aim to investigate how subjects’ knowledge and mental representations, generated through a series of tasks, influence transfer and thus affect their problem-solving performance in subsequent tasks. To prevent subject's from trying to diving too much into the intricies of this study, this is all the information we are going to disclose at the moment.

Requirements

You can participate in this study if you are between 20 and 50 years of age. Selected age group will be filtered out afterwards, so we encourage you to participate nonetheless for the fun of the experimentation.

Suggested preparations

The experriments should take between 15 and 40 minutes to complete, averaging around 20 minutes. Before you start you should remove all distractions and make yourself as comfortable and relaxed as possible. You should try to perform your best during the experiment, but keep in mind to remain calm and relaxed.

Procedure

Our study is split into two experiments, titled Matchstick experiment and Familiar figures experiment, which can be attempted in any order. Before attempting any of them, you will be asked in input some personal data and we urge you to provide real values. In the familiar-figures experiment, you will be asked to match similar images, and in the matchstick experiment, you will be asked to solve equations build up of matchsticks. In the matchstick experiment you will be placed into one of four groups, but you will not be told into which one.

Personal data

Some personal data will be acquired for the purpose of the experiment and for the purpose of tracking subjects to prevent fraud. Experimental data (performance during experiments) and basic personal data [age and sex] will be stored on a secure server accessible only to experimentators. Sensitive personal data, such as IP addresses and locations, will be stored in an encrypted fashion. Subjects cannot be tracked even in a remote event of data breach. Any published data will not contain any personal information. Only aggregated data.
